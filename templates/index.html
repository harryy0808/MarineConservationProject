Endangered Species in the Ocean

North Atlantic Right Whale

By 2021, the NOrth Atlantic right whale population was estimated to have decreased down to fewever than 340 individuals, mainly caused by ship strikes and entanglement in fishing gear.

Southern Bluefin Tuna

This fish species has recently moved from being listed as a "critically endangered" species to "endangered." However, the threat of overfishing remains as some tuna supplies are still depleted in certain regions. Rising ocean temperature is also a challenge for the Southern Bluefin Tuna since it affects their respiratory and circulatory systems.

Giant Manta Ray

A 2020 IUCN report found that giant manta ray had undergone a population decline of between 50 percent and 79 percent within 87 years, which was a direct consequence of unsustainable fishing practicies. Gaint manta rays are targeted and harvested for their gill plates, which they use to filter feed on small zooplankton from the water body, especially in the market in Asia.
